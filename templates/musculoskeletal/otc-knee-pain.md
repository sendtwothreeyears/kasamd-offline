# OTC KNEE PAIN

**Category:** Musculoskeletal & Rheumatology
**Source:** https://www.soapnote.org/musculoskeletal/otc-knee-pain/

---

Employee presents to the window with a specific request for Aleve  
Allergies: NKDA
Current Medications: lisinopril - according to EE Aleve is okay to take in B/P meds 
Medical condition (Reason for request): Calf pain/Swelling 
Medication was self-selected by employees. Medication directions, warnings, and directions were available to employees at the time of the request
General: Well appearing, well-nourished, in no distress. Oriented x 3, normal mood and affect. 
Stabile Gait denies pain when walking. 
Inspection Calf: Presence swelling +2 distal medial R calf, proximal to R ankle. 
Inspection Feet: Document bounding artery pulses and <3 capillary refill. + Sensation. Range of motion (full bilateral). Flexion to (full bilateral). Abduction to (full bilateral). Internal Rotation (full bilateral). External rotation (full bilateral). Strength (full bilateral).
Palpation: (Tenderness) to palpation mid-calf, radiates to ankle).
Recommended EE follow-up with PCP for further evaluation.
